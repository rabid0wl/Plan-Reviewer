## Glossary of Defined Terms

### 66323 Units
One of the four delineated types of ADUs (and JADUs) that must be permitted pursuant to Government Code section 66323. **Source:** Gov. Code § 66323.

### Accessory Dwelling Unit (ADU)
An attached or detached residential dwelling unit that provides complete, independent living facilities for one or more persons, located on a lot with a proposed or existing primary residence. Includes permanent provisions for living, sleeping, eating, cooking, and sanitation. Also includes: (A) an efficiency unit; (B) a manufactured home (HSC, § 18007). **Source:** Gov. Code § 66313, subd. (a).

### Accessory Structure
A structure that is accessory and incidental to a dwelling located on the same lot. **Source:** Gov. Code § 66313, subd. (b).

### Applicant
The person or party responsible for the submittal of an application seeking a permit.

### Application
A formal request to perform work, which includes all information as required.

### Certificate of Occupancy
A document issued by local Building and Safety departments certifying that a commercial space or newly constructed residential building has been inspected for compliance with the California Building Standards Code and local ordinances. **Source:** 2022 California Residential Code § R110.

### Common Interest Development
Any of: (a) a community apartment project, (b) a condominium project, (c) a planned development, (d) a stock cooperative. **Source:** Civ. Code § 4100.

### Covenants, Conditions, and Restrictions (CC&Rs)
A set of rules governing the use of real estate in each community. "Governing documents" includes declarations, bylaws, operating rules, articles of incorporation or association governing the operation of the common interest development or association. **Source:** Civ. Code § 4150.

### Efficiency Dwelling Unit
A dwelling unit with a minimum of 150 square feet of living space, a separate closet, kitchen sink, cooking appliance, refrigerator, and a separate bathroom with water closet, lavatory, and bathtub or shower. **Source:** Gov. Code § 66313, subd. (a)(1); CBC § 1208.4.

### Habitable Space
A space in a building for living, sleeping, eating, or cooking. Bathrooms, toilet rooms, closets, halls, storage or utility spaces, and similar areas are **not** habitable spaces. **Source:** 2022 CBC § 202.

### High Quality Transit Corridor
A corridor with fixed route bus service with service intervals no longer than 15 minutes during peak commute hours. **Source:** PRC § 21155, subd. (b).

### Impact Fee
Same meaning as "fee" in Gov. Code § 66000, subd. (b), including fees in § 66477. Does **not** include connection fees or capacity charges charged by a local agency, special district, or water corporation. **Source:** Gov. Code § 66324, subd. (c)(2).

### Interior Livable Space
A measurement basis used for ADU and JADU size thresholds. Size limitations, fee exemptions, and assessable space calculations are based on the square footage of interior livable space. (Changed by SB 543, effective January 1, 2026.) **Source:** Gov. Code §§ 66311.5, 66313(d), 66321(b). See also: **Livable Space**.

### Junior Accessory Dwelling Unit (JADU)
A unit no more than 500 square feet of **interior livable space** in size, contained entirely within a single-family residence. May include separate sanitation facilities or share them with the existing structure. (Changed by SB 543, effective January 1, 2026.) **Source:** Gov. Code § 66313, subd. (d).

### Livable Space
A space in a dwelling intended for human habitation, including living, sleeping, eating, cooking, or sanitation. **Source:** Gov. Code § 66313, subd. (e).

### Living Area
The interior habitable area of a dwelling unit, including basements and attics, but **not** a garage or any accessory structure. **Source:** Gov. Code § 66313, subd. (f).

### Local Agency
A city, county, or city and county, whether general law or chartered. **Source:** Gov. Code § 66313, subd. (g).

### Major Transit Stop
A site containing: (a) an existing rail or bus rapid transit station, (b) a ferry terminal served by bus or rail transit, or (c) the intersection of two or more major bus routes with frequency of 15 minutes or less during morning and afternoon peak commute periods. **Source:** PRC § 21064.3.

### Manufactured Home
A structure constructed on or after June 15, 1976, transportable in one or more sections, 8+ body feet wide or 40+ body feet long (traveling mode) or 320+ sq ft (erected), built on a permanent chassis, designed for single-family dwelling use. **Source:** HSC § 18007, subd. (a).

### Ministerial Approval
Considered and approved without discretionary review or a hearing. **Source:** Gov. Code § 66317, subd. (a).

### Multifamily Dwelling
A structure with two or more attached dwellings on a single lot. Multiple detached single-family dwellings on the same lot are **not** considered multifamily for ADU purposes.

### Nonconforming Zoning Condition
A physical improvement on a property that does not conform with current zoning standards. **Source:** Gov. Code § 66313, subd. (h).

### Objective Standards
Standards that involve no personal or subjective judgment by a public official and are uniformly verifiable by reference to an external and uniform benchmark or criterion available and knowable by both the applicant and the public official prior to submittal. **Source:** Gov. Code § 66313, subd. (i).

### Passageway
A pathway that is unobstructed, clear to the sky, and extends from a street to one entrance of the ADU. **Source:** Gov. Code § 66313, subd. (j).

### Permitting Agency
Any entity involved in the review of a permit for an ADU or JADU for which there is no substitute, including planning departments, building departments, utilities, and special districts. **Source:** Gov. Code § 66313, subd. (k).

### Planned Development
A real property development (other than community apartment project, condominium project, or stock cooperative) having: (a) common area owned by an association or in common by separate interest owners, and/or (b) common area with an association that maintains it with power to levy assessments. **Source:** Civ. Code § 4175.

### Proposed Dwelling
A dwelling that is the subject of a permit application and meets the requirements for permitting. **Source:** Gov. Code § 66313, subd. (l).

### Public Transit
A location (bus stop, train station, etc.) where the public may access buses, trains, subways, and other forms of transportation that charge set fares, run on fixed routes, and are available to the public. **Source:** Gov. Code § 66313, subd. (m).

### Qualified Buyer
Persons and families of low or moderate income (HSC, § 50093). **Source:** Gov. Code § 66340, subd. (a).

### Qualified Nonprofit Corporation
A nonprofit organized under IRC § 501(c)(3) that has received a welfare exemption under Revenue and Taxation Code § 214.15 for properties intended to be sold to low-income families in a special no-interest loan program. **Source:** Gov. Code § 66340, subd. (b).

### Reasonable Restrictions
Restrictions that do not unreasonably increase the cost to construct, effectively prohibit the construction of, or extinguish the ability to otherwise construct an ADU or JADU. **Source:** Civ. Code §§ 4751, subd. (b); 714.3, subd. (b).

### Single-Family Dwelling
A single unit providing complete, independent living facilities for one family, including permanent provisions for living, sleeping, eating, cooking, and sanitation, existing as a separate interest of real property (e.g., detached single-family dwelling or townhouse).

### Substandard Building
Any building, portion thereof, or premises in which there exist conditions that endanger the life, limb, health, property, safety, or welfare of the public or occupants. **Source:** HSC § 17920.3.

### Tandem Parking
Two or more automobiles parked on a driveway or other location on a lot, lined up behind one another. **Source:** Gov. Code § 66313, subd. (n).
