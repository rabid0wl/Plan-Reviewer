## 1. ADU/JADU Page Content

**URL:** https://www.placentia.org/1133/Accessory-Dwelling-Units-ADUJADU

### Overview
Placentia allows ADUs and JADUs in residential neighborhoods under Municipal Code Chapter 23.73, in compliance with state law. The city describes these units as providing affordable housing options and enabling intergenerational living.

### ADU Types Described on City Page

| Type | Key Details |
|------|------------|
| **Detached ADU** | Standalone structure, up to 1,200 sq ft, 16-20 ft height, min 4 ft setbacks. Fee-exempt if under 750 sq ft. |
| **Attached ADU** | Shares wall with primary home; up to 25 ft or zoning limit. Cannot exceed 50% of existing living area or 1,200 sq ft. |
| **Conversion ADU** | Repurpose existing spaces (garages, accessory structures) into independent units with own entrance. |
| **JADU (Junior ADU)** | Under 500 sq ft, must be within existing or proposed single-family home; efficiency kitchen required; may share bathroom with primary dwelling. |

### Key Rules Highlighted on City Page
- Cannot be used for short-term rental of less than 30 days
- No additional parking required within 0.5 miles of public transit
- Fire sprinklers required only if mandated for primary dwelling
- New detached structures must install solar systems (California Energy Code)
- ADUs under 750 sq ft are exempt from development impact fees

### Application Process (Per City Page)
1. Determine property eligibility
2. Define needs and budget
3. Evaluate lot compatibility
4. Design unit
5. Hire contractor
6. Submit to Planning Department with required documentation
7. Obtain Building Permit

- **Submittal Requirements:** https://placentia.org/DocumentCenter/View/12261/Submittal-Requirements---ADU-JADU
- **Pre-Approved Plans Portal:** https://www.placentia.org/1134/Preapproved-Accessory-Dwelling-ADU-Plans
- **Energy Code Fact Sheet:** https://www.placentia.org/DocumentCenter/View/14797/

## 3. Municipal Code Chapter 23.73

**URL:** https://ecode360.com/42706480
**Code Version Date:** 2024-09-17
**Recent Amendment:** Ord. O-2025-05, adopted 2025-07-15 — "Amends Zoning: Accessory and Junior Accessory Dwelling Units"

### Section 23.73.010 — Purpose
Establishes ministerial regulations consistent with Government Code Sections 65852.2 and 65852.22 to:
1. Expand the mix of housing opportunities by encouraging ADUs
2. Maintain the character of residential neighborhoods

**State law preemption:** If there is any inconsistency between this chapter and mandatory requirements of state law, state law shall control.

### Section 23.73.020 — Definitions

- **"Accessory dwelling unit"** — A residential dwelling unit which is attached, detached, or located within a single-family residence, providing complete independent living facilities for one or more persons. Includes permanent provisions for living, sleeping, eating, cooking, and sanitation on the same parcel. Also includes: (1) efficiency unit per HSC 17958.1; (2) manufactured home per HSC 18007.
- **"Junior accessory dwelling unit"** — A unit that is no more than 500 sq ft and contained entirely within a single-family residence.

### Section 23.73.030 — Ministerial Review
- Applications shall be ministerially processed within **60 days** of receipt of a complete application
- If submitted with a new single-family dwelling application, the ADU application shall not be acted upon until the SFR is approved, then ministerially approved within 60 days
- City shall grant a delay if requested by the applicant

### Section 23.73.040 — Permitted Use Zones
ADUs and JADUs are a ministerially permitted use in:
1. R-A (Residential Agricultural)
2. R-1 (Single-Family Residential)
3. R-2 (Low-Medium Density Multiple Family)
4. R-G (Medium Density Multiple-Family)
5. R-3 (High Density Multiple-Family)
6. RPC (Residential Planned Community)
7. PUD (Planned Unit Development)
8. Specific Plan 1 (areas where residential use is allowed)
9. Specific Plan 2 (areas where residential use is allowed)
10. Specific Plan 4 (areas where residential use is allowed)
11. Specific Plan 6 (areas where residential use is allowed)
12. Specific Plan 7 (areas where residential use is allowed)
13. Specific Plan 8 (areas where residential use is allowed)
14. Specific Plan 9 (areas where residential use is allowed)
15. Specific Plan 10 (areas where residential use is allowed)
16. Transit Oriented Development Packing House (areas where residential use is allowed)
17. Old Town Placentia Revitalization Plan (areas where residential use is allowed)

### Section 23.73.050 — Submittal Requirements

**Fees:** Applicant must pay all required fees set by city council, including staff review, permit, and inspection fees.

**Application Contents:**
- **Plot Plan (Drawn to Scale):** Three (3) sets of plans including plot plans, elevations, and landscape plans showing:
  - Lots, setbacks, rights-of-way
  - Building envelopes of current and proposed buildings
  - Paved areas
  - Location, size, height, materials, colors, and proposed use
  - Yards and space between buildings
  - Walls and fences (location, height, materials, color)
  - Off-street parking (location, number of spaces, dimensions)
  - Grading and drainage plans

- **Floor Plans:** Complete floor plans of existing and proposed conditions with room dimensions, floor area calculations, labeled room uses, door/closet/wall/cooking facility details.

- **Elevations:** North, south, east, and west elevations showing all exterior structure dimensions, architectural projections, and openings for both existing residence and proposed ADU.

### Section 23.73.060 — Development Standards

#### (a) Underlying Zoning
ADUs must comply with all development standards for the zone (setbacks, height, lot coverage) unless modified by this chapter.

#### (b) Specific Standards

**Floorspace:**
- Attached ADU: max 50% of primary dwelling living area or 1,200 sq ft, whichever is LESS
- Detached ADU: max 1,200 sq ft
- Minimum: must allow an efficiency unit per HSC 17958.1

**Setbacks:**
- Front yard: comply with zone requirement
- Side and rear: 4 feet
- Garage/accessory structure conversions: no setback beyond existing structure

**Distance Between Buildings:**
- Minimum 10 feet between detached ADU and primary dwelling and all other structures

**Height:**
- Detached ADU: max 20 feet
- Attached ADU: same as primary structure height requirements

**State-Mandated Minimum (Section b(5)):**
- Applicant is entitled to build an 800 sq ft ADU behind the front yard setback, max 20 ft height, with 4 ft side and rear setbacks — regardless of other standards.

**Architecture:**
- Must match architectural style of primary dwelling
- Building materials, colors, and exterior finishes must be substantially the same
- Entrance shall not be clearly visible from the street or on same side as primary entrance

**Parking:**
- General: 1 off-street parking space required (may be covered or uncovered)
- May be tandem on existing driveway (blocking max 1 other required space)
- May be in setback areas
- No replacement parking required when garage/carport is demolished or converted
- **Parking exemptions** (no space required if):
  - Within 1/2 mile walking distance of public transit (bus stop, train station)
  - Within architecturally/historically significant historic district
  - Part of existing primary residence or existing accessory structure
  - On-street parking permits required but not offered to ADU occupant
  - Car share vehicle within 1 block

**Entrances:**
- Max 1 exterior entrance on any one side of the ADU

**Stairways:**
- No exterior stairways clearly visible from any adjacent street

**Utilities:**
- All utility installations underground
- Adequate water and sewer service required
- Conversion ADUs within existing structures: NO new/separate utility connection required, NO connection fee or capacity charge
- All other ADUs: new/separate utility connection REQUIRED, proportionate connection fee/capacity charge based on size or plumbing fixtures

**Other:**
- Must be on a legally subdivided lot
- One ADU per residential lot (except as otherwise allowed)
- Fire sprinklers not required if not required in primary unit
- ADU within existing structure: exempt from lot coverage, max height, parking, and setback (only fire safety minimum)
- ADU does not count toward density calculations

### Section 23.73.070 — Additional Requirements

**Owner Occupancy:**
- If different families in primary unit and ADU, one family must include property owner
- **Exception:** Does NOT apply to ADUs approved between January 1, 2020 and January 1, 2025

**Additions:**
- Any additions to an ADU must meet chapter requirements

**Impact Fees:**
- ADUs under 750 sq ft: NO impact fees
- ADUs 750 sq ft or greater: fees charged proportionately relative to primary dwelling square footage
- All applicable public service and recreation impact fees paid prior to occupancy
- "Impact fee" has same meaning as Government Code Section 65852.2(f)

**Deed Restriction (Required before permit):**
- File with county recorder, approved by city attorney
- ADU shall not be sold separately from primary dwelling
- ADU restrictions are binding on all successors in ownership
- Non-compliance results in legal action
- If owner-occupancy applies, covenant must state that requirement

**Enforcement (Until January 1, 2030):**
- City shall issue a statement with notice to correct violations relating to ADU
- Owner may file application if they believe correction is not necessary for public health/safety
- City may delay enforcement for 5 years if ADU does not present threat

**Short-Term Rental:**
- ADU shall not be rented for less than 30 consecutive calendar days
- If rental term less than 30 days is requested, written documentation required showing special circumstances and necessity

### Section 23.73.080 — Mandatory Approvals

The city must ministerially approve:

**(1) Within existing single-family:**
- One ADU or JADU within existing space of single-family home or existing accessory structure
- ADU has independent exterior access
- Side and rear setbacks sufficient for fire and safety
- JADU must comply with Section 23.73.090

**(2) Small detached ADU on single-family lot:**
- Max 800 sq ft
- Max 16 ft height
- 4 ft side and rear yard setbacks
- A JADU may also be developed on the same lot

**(3) On multifamily lot (conversion):**
- Up to 25% of total multifamily units (min 1 unit) within existing non-livable space (storage, boiler rooms, passageways, attics, basements, garages)
- Each unit must comply with state building standards

**(4) On multifamily lot (detached):**
- Up to 2 detached units
- Max 16 ft height
- 4 ft side and rear setbacks

**Note:** For mandatory approval ADUs, city shall not require correction of legal, nonconforming zoning conditions.

### Section 23.73.090 — Junior Accessory Dwelling Units

- One JADU allowed per single-family lot with existing or proposed SFR
- JADU may coexist with detached ADU if ADU is max 800 sq ft and max 16 ft
- Must contain efficiency kitchen (cooking appliances, food prep counter, storage cabinets)
- Separate entrance from primary residence required
- Separate sanitation facilities optional (if not provided, must share with SFR and have interior access)
- No additional parking required
- Must comply with applicable building standards

**Deed Restriction Required:**
1. No separate sale from primary residence
2. Cannot be enlarged beyond 500 sq ft
3. No rental less than 31 consecutive calendar days
4. Owner must reside in either primary or JADU (exceptions for governmental agencies, land trusts, housing organizations; or renting both to one party with no sublease)
5. Deed restrictions enforceable against future purchasers; copy filed with development department

**Utilities:**
- For fire/life protection, service water, sewer, or power (including connection fees): JADU is NOT considered a separate or new dwelling unit

**Nonconforming Conditions:**
- For mandatory approval ADUs, city shall not require correction of legal, nonconforming zoning conditions

**Fees:**
- Applicant must pay all required fees set by city council

**URL:** https://www.placentia.org/372/Exceptions

- Painting, papering, and similar interior finishes
- Temporary movie/theater sets
- Residential window awnings projecting under 54 inches
- Above-ground pools under 5,000 gallons (residential)

**Note:** Plumbing, electrical, and mechanical permits still required for exempt items. No ADU-specific exemptions listed.

## 12. Third-Party ADU Analyses

### GatherADU
**URL:** https://www.gatheradu.com/adu-info/placentia-ca

Key points:
- Max ADU size: 1,200 sq ft
- JADU: up to 500 sq ft
- Detached max height: 16 ft (note: municipal code actually says 20 ft for standard, 16 ft for mandatory approval)
- 4 ft side and rear setbacks
- 1 ADU + 1 JADU allowed per single-family lot
- Estimated permit timeline: ~60 days
- Full project timeline: 10-14 months (feasibility/design 2-3 mo, permitting 2-3 mo, construction 4-8 mo)
- Design review and architectural standards required
- Garage conversions: no replacement parking required

### Dwellito

Key points:
- Max 1,200 sq ft
- 4 ft side/rear setbacks
- 16 ft minimum height (detached)
- 0-1 parking spaces
- 1-2 units per property
- No minimum lot size
- Residential zones only
- SB9 lot splits eligible
