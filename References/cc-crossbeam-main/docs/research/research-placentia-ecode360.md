## 1. ADU Ordinance -- Chapter 23.73: Accessory and Junior Accessory Dwelling Units

**ecode360 URL:** https://ecode360.com/42706480
**City ADU Page:** https://www.placentia.org/1133/Accessory-Dwelling-Units-ADUJADU

### 1.2 Purpose (Section 23.73.010)

Chapter 23.73 is intended to establish ministerial regulations consistent with Government Code Sections 65852.2 and 65852.22, to properly balance the city's goals of:
- Expanding the mix of housing opportunities by encouraging the establishment of ADUs
- Maintaining the character of residential neighborhoods by regulating when and how ADUs may be built and operated

### 1.3 Permitted Locations (Section 23.73.040)

ADUs and JADUs are a **ministerially permitted use** in:
- Any **single-family** zone
- Any **multi-family** zone
- Any **mixed-use** zone
- Areas of **Specific Plans** and other areas that allow for residential uses

This includes zones R-A, R-1, R-2, R-G, R-3, RPC, PUD, and applicable Specific Plan areas.

### 1.4 Number of Units Permitted (Section 23.73.050)

#### Single-Family Lots
- **1 ADU** (attached or detached) per lot with an existing or proposed single-family dwelling
- **1 JADU** in addition to 1 ADU (i.e., up to **1 ADU + 1 JADU** per single-family lot)

#### Multi-Family Lots
- **Conversions:** Up to **25% of existing multifamily dwelling units** (but no less than 1 unit) may be converted to ADUs within portions of existing structures not used as livable space (storage rooms, boiler rooms, passageways, attics, basements, garages), provided each unit complies with state building standards for dwellings
- **New detached ADUs:** Up to **2 detached ADUs** on a lot with a multifamily dwelling structure, provided:
  - Neither unit exceeds **16 feet** in height
  - Each has at least **4-foot** side and rear yard setbacks

### 1.5 Development Standards (Section 23.73.060)

ADUs shall comply with all development standards for a new residential dwelling unit in the zone in which the ADU is to be located, including setbacks, height, and lot coverage, **unless otherwise addressed by this chapter or contrary to state law**.

#### 1.5.1 Size Limits

| ADU Type | Minimum | Maximum |
|----------|---------|---------|
| Detached ADU | 150 sq ft (per state law) | **1,200 sq ft** |
| Attached ADU | 150 sq ft (per state law) | **50% of existing primary dwelling living area** OR **1,200 sq ft**, whichever is less |
| Conversion ADU (within existing structure) | N/A | Limited by existing structure footprint |
| JADU | N/A | **500 sq ft** |

- ADUs must include sleeping, cooking, bathroom/bathing, and living areas (complete independent living facilities)

#### 1.5.2 Height Limits

| ADU Type | Maximum Height |
|----------|---------------|
| Detached ADU (on SF lot, by-right under state law) | **16 feet** (for up to 800 sq ft units) |
| Detached ADU (general, per local ordinance) | **20 feet** |
| Detached ADU on multifamily lot | **16 feet** |
| Attached ADU | **25 feet** or the height limit in the underlying zone, **whichever is lower**; may be up to **2 stories** |
| Conversion ADU (existing structure) | No additional height limit applies |
| Above-garage ADU | **18-20 feet** possible depending on setback distance |

> **Note on the 800 sq ft / 16 ft by-right provision:** Per state law (Gov. Code 65852.2), one detached ADU of up to 800 sq ft and 16 feet in height with 4-foot setbacks must be approved ministerially regardless of local standards. The local Placentia code allows up to 1,200 sq ft and 20 feet for detached ADUs that meet all local standards.

#### 1.5.3 Setback Requirements

| ADU Type | Front | Side | Rear |
|----------|-------|------|------|
| Detached ADU (new construction) | Per underlying zone | **4 ft** minimum | **4 ft** minimum |
| Attached ADU | Per underlying zone | **4 ft** minimum | **4 ft** minimum |
| Conversion of existing garage/structure | No setback required beyond existing | No setback required beyond existing | No setback required beyond existing |
| JADU (within existing home) | N/A | N/A | N/A |

- Side and rear setbacks shall be **sufficient for fire and safety**
- If an existing garage or accessory structure is converted to an ADU, or an ADU is built in the same location and to the same dimensions, then **no setback is required** beyond that which exists for the existing garage or accessory structure
- Front yard and street-side setbacks may require greater than 4 feet (per underlying zone)

#### 1.5.4 Parking Requirements

| Situation | Parking Required |
|-----------|-----------------|
| General ADU (attached or detached) | **1 off-street parking space** |
| ADU within **1/2 mile walking distance** of public transit | **0 spaces** (exempt) |
| ADU within a historic district | **0 spaces** (exempt) |
| ADU within 1 block of a car-share vehicle | **0 spaces** (exempt) |
| Conversion ADU (garage/carport conversion) | **0 spaces** (no replacement parking required) |
| JADU | **0 spaces** |

- When a garage, carport, or covered parking structure is demolished in conjunction with ADU construction, or converted to an ADU, the city **shall not require those off-street parking spaces be replaced**

#### 1.5.5 Design Standards

- The ADU shall be **compatible with and complement the architectural style** of the primary dwelling
- The **entrance** to the ADU shall **not be clearly visible from the street** adjacent to the property, or on the same side as the entrance to the primary dwelling unit
- Design and location standards (setbacks, lot coverage, tree preservation, entrance placements) **cannot be stricter** than those for the main home

#### 1.5.6 Utility Connections

- **ADU within existing single-family home or existing accessory structure:** The city shall **not** require installation of a new or separate utility connection, and shall **not** impose a connection fee or capacity charge
- **All other ADUs (new construction):** The city **shall** require a new or separate utility connection. Connection fee or capacity charge must be **proportionate** to the burden of the proposed ADU, based on the size or number of plumbing fixtures

#### 1.5.7 Fire Sprinklers

- Fire sprinklers **shall not be required** in any ADU if they were not required in the primary unit

#### 1.5.8 Solar Requirements

- Newly constructed, non-manufactured, detached ADU structures are subject to the **California Energy Code** requirement to install solar systems (Title 24 Part 6)

### 1.6 Sale/Rental Restrictions

- ADU **shall not be used** for short-term rental of **less than 30 days**
- ADU may not be sold separately from the primary residence (per state law; Placentia has not opted into AB 1033 as of this research)
- ADUs and JADUs have the **same short-term rental limitations** as the primary residence

### 1.7 JADU-Specific Rules (Section 23.73.090)

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| Maximum size | **500 sq ft** |
| Location | Must be **entirely contained within** an existing or proposed single-family residence |
| Kitchen | Required, but **efficiency kitchen** standards apply (full-size appliances not mandatory; sink, cooking facility, and food prep counter required) |
| Bathroom | A dedicated bathroom is **not required** (may share with primary residence) |
| Entrance | Must have an **independent entrance** |
| Parking | **None required** |
| Owner occupancy | **Required** -- owner must occupy either the main house or the JADU |
| Deed restriction | A **deed restriction** is required for JADUs, recorded against the property, indicating owner-occupancy requirement |
| Separate sale | **Cannot be sold** separately from the primary dwelling |
| Short-term rental | **Prohibited** (less than 30 days) |

### 1.8 Permit Processing (Section 23.73.080)

- Applications for ADUs and JADUs shall be **ministerially processed** within **60 days** of receipt of a complete application
- Approved if they meet the requirements of Chapter 23.73
- No discretionary review, public hearing, or environmental review required

### 1.9 Impact Fees

| ADU Size | Impact Fee |
|----------|-----------|
| **Less than 750 sq ft** | **Exempt** from development impact fees |
| **750 sq ft or more** (on SF lot) | Proportional: (Full SF DIF) x (ADU sq ft / Primary dwelling sq ft) |
| **750 sq ft or more** (on MF lot) | Proportional: (Full MF DIF) x (ADU sq ft / Average MF unit sq ft) |
| **JADU within existing SF home** | **Exempt** from citywide DIFs |
| **JADU as new construction or addition** | Subject to DIFs at the **same rate as SF residence** |

| Standard | Requirement |

| **Minimum frontage** | No less than **40 feet** at property line |

#### Accessory Structure Rules (General Regulations, Chapter 23.81)

**ecode360 URL:** https://ecode360.com/42706938

- Accessory buildings **may not** be constructed prior to the establishment of the main building
- An accessory building in an "R" district shall be located on the **rear one-half** of the lot
- Must be at least **10 feet** from any dwelling building existing or under construction on the same lot or any adjacent lot
- Shall not be within **5 feet** of any alley
- Shall not be within **1 foot** of the side line of the lot
- Corner lot: shall not project beyond the front yard required or existing on the adjacent lot
- Maximum height for accessory buildings: **20 feet**

### 2.8 Definitions (Chapter 23.04)

**ecode360 URL:** https://ecode360.com/42705019

Key definitions:
- **Accessory Dwelling Unit (ADU):** A residential dwelling unit which is attached, detached, or located within a single-family residence, which provides complete independent living facilities for one or more persons
- **Junior Accessory Dwelling Unit (JADU):** A unit that is no more than 500 sq ft and contained entirely within a single-family residence

### 2.9 Two-Unit Housing Developments (Chapter 23.69)

**ecode360 URL:** https://ecode360.com/42705356

- Implements SB 9 for two-unit (duplex) projects and urban lot splits on single-family zoned parcels
- Development standards must be objective (no subjective style/design review)
- Lot splits: Cannot exceed 60/40 split

## 4. Fire Code -- Title 18: Fire Protection

**ecode360 URL:** https://ecode360.com/42703136

### 4.1 Fire Service Provider

- **Placentia Fire and Life Safety Department** -- city-operated department (NOT OCFA)
- Began operations: **July 1, 2020**
- **Two fire stations:**
  - Station 1: 110 South Bradford Avenue
  - Station 2: 1530 North Valencia Avenue
- Dedicated career firefighters providing 24-hour emergency response
- Fire Chief: Jason Dobine, (714) 993-8135
- Community Risk Reduction email: FireCRR@placentia.org

### 4.2 Fire Code Adoption (Chapter 18.04)

| Standard | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Code adopted** | California Fire Code (CFC), **2022 Edition** |
| **Base code** | 2021 International Fire Code (IFC) |
| **Purpose** | Safeguard life and property from fire/explosion hazards |
| **Enforcement** | Local enforcement agency (Placentia Fire and Life Safety Department) |
| **Local amendments** | Yes -- included as attachment (Section 18.04.050) |

### 4.3 Local Fire Code Amendments (Section 18.04.050)

Key local amendments include:
- **Fire department access and water requirements** per City of Placentia Guideline for Fire Department Access & Water Requirements for Commercial & Residential Development
- Restrictions on **use or storage of extremely hazardous substances (EHS)** in residential zoned or residentially developed properties
- Requirements for **Hazardous Materials Inventory Statements (HMIS)**

### 4.4 Sprinkler Requirements for ADUs

- Fire sprinklers **shall not be required** in any ADU if they were not required in the primary unit (per PMC 23.73 and state law)
- For new construction primary residences: per CFC/CRC standards (generally required for new construction)

### 4.5 Fire Hazard Severity Zones

- On **March 24, 2025**, CAL FIRE's Office of the State Fire Marshal (OSFM) issued the 2025 recommended LRA FHSZ maps, and **Placentia was newly added**
- On **June 17, 2025**, the City Council adopted **Ordinance No. O-2025-04**, designating Fire Hazard Severity Zones
- Local agency **cannot decrease** the level of the LRA FHSZ recommended by OSFM; may only maintain or increase
- Interactive 2025 FHSZ map available on city website
- **Impact:** Homes in FHSZ may require prescribed home hardening features to withstand wildfire impact

**City FHSZ Page:** https://www.placentia.org/1148/New-Local-Responsibility-Area-LRA-Fire-H

## 5. Fees

### 5.1 Development Impact Fees

**City Fees Page:** https://www.placentia.org/778/Development-Fees
**Fee Schedule Page:** https://www.placentia.org/788/Fee-Schedule

- Current fee schedule adopted **April 15, 2025** per Resolution No. R-2025-13

- **Fee documents:**
  - City Development Fees and Outside Agency Fees (updated 6-13-24)

#### ADU Impact Fee Structure

| ADU Scenario | Fee |
|-------------|-----|
| ADU < 750 sq ft | **No development impact fees** |
| ADU >= 750 sq ft on SF lot | (Full SF DIF) x (ADU sq ft / Primary dwelling sq ft) |
| ADU >= 750 sq ft on MF lot | (Full MF DIF) x (ADU sq ft / Avg MF unit sq ft) |
| JADU within existing SF home | **No citywide DIFs** |
| JADU as new construction/addition | Full SF DIF rate |

> **Note:** Specific dollar amounts for DIFs were not accessible via PDF download. Contact City at (714) 993-8117 for current fee amounts.

### 5.2 Affordable Housing Fees (Chapter 5.30)

**ecode360 URL:** https://ecode360.com/42698247

- Housing impact fees collected are deposited in a **separate housing impact fee fund**
- Funds used exclusively to provide housing affordable to extremely low, very low, lower, and moderate income households
- **Exemptions:** Residential development projects of **4 or fewer new housing units** (or creation of 4 or fewer parcels with no more than 4 dwelling units) are **exempt**
- Replacement construction resulting in a net increase of 4 or fewer units is also **exempt**
- Annual fee adjustments based on April-to-April **Construction Cost Index for Los Angeles** (Engineering News-Record)

### 5.3 Plan Check and Building Permit Fees

- All applicable plan check and development impact fees apply to ADU projects
- Pre-approved ADU plans still require payment of all applicable fees
- Three sets of 24"x36" plans required for submittal

## 6. Utilities

### 6.1 Water Service

| Provider | Coverage |
|----------|----------|
| **Golden State Water Company** (Placentia/Yorba Linda system) | **Majority** of city residential water service |
| **Yorba Linda Water District** | Small portion of city residential service |

- Golden State Water Company (subsidiary of American States Water Company / AWR) established 1929
- Water sources: Blend of **groundwater** (Orange County Groundwater Basin) and **imported water** (Colorado River Aqueduct and State Water Project via Metropolitan Water District of Southern California)

**Golden State Water - Placentia:** https://www.gswater.com/placentia

### 6.2 Sewer Service

| Entity | Role |
|--------|------|
| **City of Placentia** | Operates and maintains **84 miles** of gravity sanitary sewer pipelines |
| **Orange County Sanitation District (OC San)** | Receives, conveys, treats, and disposes of wastewater |

- City's wastewater collection system conveys untreated wastewater to OCSD trunk sewer system via **35 separate connections**
- OCSD treats and disposes of city wastewater flows via OCSD treatment plants
- Sewer Fee (city) is **separate** from OCSD charge on property tax bill

**City Sewer Info:** https://www.placentia.org/259/Sanitary-Sewer-Maintenance

### 6.3 Stormwater / NPDES

**City Stormwater Page:** https://www.placentia.org/262/Stormwater-NPDES

- Stormwater regulations codified in **Title 16, Chapter 16.20** of the Municipal Code
- City operates under **NPDES Permits** (National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System)
- Two drainage systems: **sanitary sewers** (indoor drains to treatment plants) and **storm drains** (rainwater to channels/rivers/ocean, untreated)
- Storm drain system carries excess rainwater to prevent flooding
- City required to effectively **prohibit non-stormwater discharges** and implement controls to reduce pollutants to **Maximum Extent Practicable (MEP)**

### 6.4 ADU Utility Connection Rules (from Chapter 23.73)

| ADU Type | Utility Connection Required | Connection Fee |
|----------|---------------------------|----------------|
| ADU within existing SF home or existing accessory structure | **No** new/separate connection required | **No** connection fee or capacity charge |
| New construction ADU | **Yes** -- new/separate connection required | **Proportionate** to burden (based on size or plumbing fixtures) |

## 7. Additional Relevant Provisions

**ecode360 URL:** https://ecode360.com/42706427
